LPAC – What You Need to Know

A Leadership Political Action Committee (LPAC) is a type of political action committee that supports candidates, policies, and initiatives aligned with its mission. LPACs play a key role in shaping political landscapes by funding campaigns, advocating for legislative changes, and mobilizing supporters.

What Does an LPAC Do?

  • Supports Political Candidates – Funds campaigns of candidates who align with its values.
  • Advocates for Policy Change – Promotes laws and reforms that benefit its cause.
  • Engages the Community – Encourages public participation in political processes.

Why Are LPACs Important?

  • They amplify voices in politics.
  • They influence key decisions and policies.
  • They help drive meaningful change in governance.

Whether you're looking to understand how LPACs operate or seeking ways to get involved, staying informed is crucial.
